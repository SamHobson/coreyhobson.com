Motorsport Games and Studio 397, in collaboration with the Automobile Club de l'Ouest (ACO), reignited the spirit of Le Mans endurance racing with the first dedicated game since 1986. Powered by the rFactor 2 engine, celebrated for its exceptional realism and a top choice among sim racing enthusiasts. The project aimed to strike a balance between high-fidelity simulation and user accessibility.

As Head of User Experience, my focus was on establishing a strong early access foundation: a platform that could evolve and grow over years of live-service development. By refining complex mechanics to be more user-friendly, the game offers an engaging and intuitive experience without sacrificing the depth and authenticity sim racers expect.

## Process

Like endurance racing itself, the time spent planning was a key contributor to the UX's success. Before there was a logo, we started with information architecture. Ensuring the game navigation matched our needs and the user flow aligned with our gameplay loops.

### Early Concepting

The main menu concept drew from moodboards and initial style investigations. Inspiration came from manufacturer marketing material, magazine articles, and advertisements built around the World Endurance Championship.

### Refined Mid-Fi

Mid-fidelity designs served as the build-off point. Final refinement came from hands-on experience with the UI in-engine.

### Final Release

The main menu needed life. Originally we aimed to use in-game renders and video, but technical constraints forced a pivot. The solution: a breathing effect paired with in-game screenshots to introduce the game while bringing atmosphere to the environment. Menu hierarchy was reworked to better apply the law of proximity and similarity.
