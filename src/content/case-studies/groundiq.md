GroundIQ exists to make ground risk transparent. Geotechs currently draw 2D cross-sections using data pulled from hundreds of meters away — sometimes kilometers — and pretend it's accurate where the line is drawn. Our opinion is that 3D is better. Everything should be grounded in a 3D model.

That's our unique selling point. Not because 3D is trendy, but because it's honest. A 3D model shows where data is missing. A cross-section hides it.

## My role

I'm Lead Product Designer. I set the North Star for product design: how we communicate to users, how we implement AI, how the product is opinionated. We don't want to be like other geotechnical software. We're 3D-focused, web-based, and built to get users to their answer quickly with automated workflows.

But our users need assurance. They need quality control points. They need to look at the data and build trust — it can't be a black box. So every automated step has a human checkpoint.

I design for the business, not just the user. That doesn't mean upselling. It means knowing that 3D modeling is our selling point, so we're opinionated and say you have to use it — but then we make damn sure it's easy to make those models. Get them to the aha moment fast.

I also translate the vision. I work closely with the Director of Product and Head of Product to turn strategy into demos, explore new ideas, inspire the team, and ultimately help with funding. I own products, features, and ways of work.

## How we work

I joined 11 months ago. We haven't used Figma once.

When code-first didn't work — branches with real data were too constrained — I built a React/HTML shell repo deployed on GitHub Pages. It looks like the platform but has no data restrictions. Anyone can see it, use it, and PR against it. It serves as both design mockup and handover document.

This is the step toward Head of Product for me. I owned features at Motorsport Games. Here I own products, set strategy, and define how a design team operates without design tools.

## The hardest problem

Two things keep me up.

**Speed vs value.** AI lets us develop fast. How do you make sure you're adding value, not just features? When everything can be built quickly, the discipline is knowing what shouldn't be built at all.

**The generation gap.** Geotechs are an older demographic. They work in PDFs that get printed and filed. There are no digital backups. No standard data formats. When we ingest project data, it arrives in Excel files — all structured differently. Every single one. We have to create standards and opinions about how things should be done, while onboarding people who've worked one way their entire careers.
