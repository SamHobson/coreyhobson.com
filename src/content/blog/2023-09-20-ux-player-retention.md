While graphics, storylines, and gameplay mechanics dominate marketing and reviews, it's UX that often decides whether a player stays engaged for 10 hours or 1,000. Retention is the metric that separates successful games from the graveyard — and UX is the discipline that drives it.

From friction-free onboarding to well-timed progression signals, from menu flow that respects mental bandwidth to error states that don't punish the player — the UX layer is where retention is won or lost. This article examines the mechanisms, with examples from both AAA and indie titles.
