Design teams don't fail because of talent. They fail because of ambiguity — unclear responsibilities, overlapping ownership, and collaboration patterns that weren't designed, just inherited.

This article is a practical guide to structuring a UX design team: defining roles (and what they don't do), building collaboration frameworks that actually work across design, engineering, and product, and creating the kind of clarity that lets designers focus on the work instead of navigating the org chart.
