In video game design, first impressions matter immensely. With a vast library of games competing for players' attention, the first 15 minutes often determine whether someone commits or uninstalls. Onboarding is the make-or-break moment.

But great onboarding isn't a tutorial that locks the player in a room for 30 minutes. It's a design philosophy that introduces mechanics through play, surfaces complexity gradually, and respects the player's intelligence. This article covers the patterns that work — and the ones that send players running.
