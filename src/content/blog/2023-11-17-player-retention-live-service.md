Online service games — "live service" games — are designed to be continually updated, offering fresh content and experiences over months and years. But a significant challenge remains: ensuring players stay engaged across seasons, updates, and the inevitable gaps between content drops.

Retention isn't just a product manager's metric. It's a UX problem. How you onboard new players, how you signal progress, how you design re-engagement triggers — these are all design decisions with direct impact on whether someone comes back tomorrow.

This article breaks down the UX patterns that drive retention in live service games, from first-time user experience to endgame loops.
