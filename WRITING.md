# Writing rules for coreyhobson.com

These apply to all content on the site: blog posts, case studies, project pages, about page.

## Voice

**Direct, not performative.** Write like you're explaining something to a colleague. No academic hedging. No passive voice when active works. "I made the call to rebuild it in React" not "The decision was made to pursue a React-based rebuild."

**Opinionated with evidence.** Every claim needs an example or data point. "GitHub's notification system is shit" works because it's followed by what specifically is broken and why. Unsupported opinions are just noise.

**"I" for experience, "you" for advice.** When describing something you did, use "I." When giving advice to the reader, use "you." Don't use "we" to blur responsibility.

## Structure

**Lead with the interesting thing.** Don't build up to it. First paragraph tells you why this matters.

**Section breaks are cliffs.** Each heading should make you want to keep reading. Not "Project Overview" — "Why we threw out Angular 0."

**End on the hard word.** No summaries. No "in conclusion." The last sentence should be the one that sticks.

## What to kill

**AI-isms.** These words are banned: delve, unlock, supercharge, game-changer, revolutionize, leverage (unless you're talking about financial leverage), empower, seamless, cutting-edge, robust. If you'd roll your eyes hearing it in a pitch meeting, cut it.

**Em-dashes.** Never. Not one. They're the punctuation of someone trying to sound thoughtful.

**Fluff adjectives.** "Comprehensive," "holistic," "strategic," "innovative" — these mean nothing without evidence. If you can't back it up with a specific, cut it.

**Process porn.** Nobody needs to read "I started with research, then moved to wireframes, then iterated based on feedback." That describes every design project ever. Show the interesting part: the specific research finding, the weird constraint, the decision that went against convention.

## Format

- Headers: sentence case. Not Title Case For Every Word.
- Lists: short. One idea per bullet.
- Links: descriptive. Not "click here." The link text should tell you where you're going.
- Images: every image gets an alt description. Every image adds information.

If a piece was significantly shaped by AI in a way that goes beyond the standard workflow, it will be noted explicitly. The default assumption for all content on this site is: human-authored, AI-assisted.
